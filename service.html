Domiciliary Care

Services

Domiciliary care is a type of care provided to individuals who need support with their daily living activities but wish to remain in their own homes. It involves a care worker visiting the individual’s home to provide care and support services.
Domiciliary care services can include assistance with personal care, such as bathing, dressing, and toileting, medication management, meal preparation, domestic tasks, companionship, and other support services tailored to the individual’s needs.

PERSONAL CARE

opportunity

Personal care is a type of domiciliary care service that is designed to provide assistance with daily living activities to elderly or disabled individuals who may require help with tasks related to personal hygiene, grooming, and mobility.
PERSONAL CARE SERVICES

  1. Bathing and showering: Assistance with getting in and out of the shower or bath, and help with washing and drying.
  2. Dressing: Assistance with dressing and undressing, and help with selecting appropriate clothing.
  3. Toileting: Assistance with using the toilet, including transferring to and from the toilet, and help with hygiene.
  4. Grooming: Assistance with grooming tasks such as shaving, hair care, and nail care.
  5. Mobility: Assistance with moving around the home, getting in and out of bed, and help with mobility aids such as wheelchairs or walkers.
  6. Skincare: Assistance with applying lotions or creams to prevent skin breakdown.
Personal care services are provided by trained and experienced caregivers who are committed to promoting independence and maintaining dignity for their clients. These services can be customized to meet the specific needs and preferences of each individual, and are an important part of ensuring that elderly or disabled individuals can continue to live comfortably and safely in their own homes.
